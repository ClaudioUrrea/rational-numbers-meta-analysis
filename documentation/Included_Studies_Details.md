# Included Studies: Detailed Descriptions

## Meta-Analysis: Technology-Enhanced Rational Number Learning in K-12

**Total Studies**: 12  
**Total N**: 1,721 students  
**Years**: 2017-2025  
**Countries**: 7 (Germany, Finland, Spain, China, UK, Taiwan, USA)

---

## Study 1: Oppmann et al. (2025)

**Full Citation**: Oppmann, M.-M., Beege, M., & Reinhold, F. (2025). Stimulating individual learning of the concept of fraction equivalence: How students utilize adaptive features in digital learning environments mediates their effect. *Learning and Instruction*, 98, 102118.

**DOI**: [10.1016/j.learninstruc.2025.102118](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.learninstruc.2025.102118)

### Study Characteristics
- **Country**: Germany
- **Sample Size**: N = 300 (Intervention: 150, Control: 150)
- **Grade Level**: 6th grade
- **Age**: ~12 years
- **Design**: Randomized Controlled Trial (RCT)
- **Journal**: *Learning and Instruction* (Q1, JCR IF: 4.2)

### Intervention
- **Technology**: Intelligent tutoring system with adaptive algorithms
- **Focus**: Fraction equivalence reasoning
- **Duration**: 6 weeks
- **Features**: Adaptive problem selection, immediate feedback, multiple representations
- **Theoretical Framework**: Cognitive Load Theory, multimedia learning principles

### Comparison
- **Control Type**: Traditional textbook instruction
- **Duration**: Equivalent (6 weeks)

### Outcomes
- **Measure**: Validated fraction equivalence test (α = 0.84)
- **Constructs**: Conceptual understanding of equivalence, multiplicative relationships
- **Assessment**: Pretest and posttest

### Results
- **Effect Size**: Hedge's g = 0.68 (SE = 0.12)
- **95% CI**: [0.447, 0.913]
- **Direction**: Favors technology intervention
- **Quality**: Strong (no weak ratings)

### Key Findings
- Adaptive features mediated learning outcomes
- Students who engaged more with adaptive scaffolding showed larger gains
- Technology particularly effective for students with lower prior knowledge

## Key Takeaways

1. **Consistent positive effects**: All 12 studies showed benefits of technology (g = 0.39 to 0.71)

2. **High methodological quality**: 50% strong quality, 50% moderate, 0% weak

3. **Diverse technologies**: Benefits observed across ITS, AR/VR, and pedagogical frameworks

4. **International evidence**: 7 countries represented across Europe, Asia, and North America

5. **Elementary-middle focus**: Critical developmental period for rational numbers (ages 8-13)

6. **Negligible heterogeneity**: I² = 0% suggests genuinely consistent effects

7. **Recent evidence**: 50% of studies published in last 3 years (2023-2025)
